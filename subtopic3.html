Adobe’s history of digital art page outlines major technological milestones such as the invention of drawing tablets, the rise of raster graphics, and the release of Photoshop. It explains how these tools transformed art by making advanced editing and illustration techniques widely accessible. The page highlights how digital tools offer flexibility that traditional media cannot, such as layers, filters, and undo actions. It also covers the emergence of AI-assisted tools, showing how the digital art landscape continues to evolve.

Visit Source Whitney Museum of American Art This museum page profiles John Whitney, a major pioneer of digital art and motion graphics. It explains how he used early computers to create generative and algorithmic animations, proving that machines could be used for creative expression. Whitney’s innovations laid the groundwork for modern digital effects, animation software, and music visualizations. His work demonstrated the artistic potential of computational systems long before digital art became mainstream, influencing countless artists and technologies that followed.
